More Info 

A report <https://pub-tdsb.escribemeetings.com/filestream.ashx?DocumentId=17569> adopted yesterday by the TDSB in a 15-7 vote <https://www.youtube.com/live/SVb3KShPlaM?feature=shared&t=21207> includes anti-Palestinian racism (APR) as an area of focus “in response to student and community voices,” and follows advocacy from Toronto Palestinian and Jewish families. This means that a working group will be formed to educate students on APR, alongside other forms of discrimination including Islamophobia and antisemitism.

A working description of anti-Palestinian racism was first developed by the Arab-Canadian Lawyers Association (ACLA) in its 2022 report titled “Anti-Palestinian Racism: Naming, Framing and Manifestations <https://static1.squarespace.com/static/61db30d12e169a5c45950345/t/627dcf83fa17ad41ff217964/1652412292220/Anti-Palestinian+Racism-+Naming%2C+Framing+and+Manifestations.pdf>.” The ACLA describes APR as “a form of anti-Arab racism that silences, excludes, erases, stereotypes, defames or dehumanizes Palestinians or their narratives,” and identifies various forms of APR, including “denying the Nakba and justifying violence against Palestinians,” “failing to acknowledge Palestinians as an Indigenous people with a collective identity,” and “defaming Palestinians and their allies with slander such as being inherently antisemitic, a terrorist threat/sympathizer or opposed to democratic values."

The need to adopt APR as part of anti-racism strategies around Canada is corroborated by the rise of violence and discrimination against Canadian Palestinians and their allies. Some recent examples of hateful acts include: An arson attack <https://london.ctvnews.ca/next-level-kind-of-hate-alleged-hate-driven-arson-concerning-for-muslims-and-police-1.6920908> on a London family in response to their lawn signs expressing support of Palestinian human rights; a mobile advertising truck <https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/toronto/toronto-anti-muslim-islamophobic-truck-advertising-1.7240534> in Toronto demonizing Muslims and Palestinians while claiming that Canada is “under siege”; beheading threats <https://winnipeg.citynews.ca/2024/06/19/winnipeg-man-says-he-believes-hes-receiving-increasing-threats-due-to-public-support-of-palestinian-community/> received by the leader of the Canadian Palestinian Association of Manitoba; and a covert foreign influence campaign <https://www.cjpme.org/pr_2024_06_05_foreign_influence> by the Israeli government spreading anti-Palestinian and anti-Muslim messages. While the APR preceded October 7, 2023, it has been on the rise within Canadian society and institutions.

Many pro-Israeli lobby groups in Canada have attacked the TDSB for this policy, and the Centre of Israel and Jewish Affairs (CIJA) even ludicrously claimed <https://www.cija.ca/tdsb_trustees_must_vote_no_chr_ignores_antisemitism> that simply recognizing anti-Palestinian racism would “result in the censorship of core aspects of Jewish identity.” Such false claims imply that anti-racism work is exclusive and a zero-sum game, and that it needs to benefit one group at the expense of another. Acknowledging anti-Palestinian hatred is a necessary part of combatting all forms of discrimination, including antisemitism, in an intersectional manner. Join us in applauding the TDSB’s decision in the hope that additional school boards, the federal government and other public bodies will follow the TDSB and incorporate APR into their anti-racism strategies <https://www.cjpme.org/tdsb_apr_policy>.
